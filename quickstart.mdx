This quickstart walks you through the complete nHungry workflow: opening the app, setting up your list, browsing products, and shopping. By the end you will have added your first product, selected it for your cart, and marked it as bought.

<Tip>
  You can use nHungry immediately without installing anything. Open [hungry.infoinnova.es](https://hungry.infoinnova.es) in any modern browser. To get the full offline experience, install it as a PWA — your browser will show an "Add to home screen" or "Install app" prompt.
</Tip>

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the app">
    Go to [hungry.infoinnova.es](https://hungry.infoinnova.es) or, if you're running locally, open [http://localhost:3000](http://localhost:3000).

    The app loads and reads your data from LocalStorage. On your first visit there is no saved data, so nHungry initializes with default values automatically.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Review the default categories and supermarkets">
    The app starts with 20 color-coded categories named **Categoría 00** through **Categoría 19**, and four default supermarkets: **Cualquier Supermercado**, **Carrefour**, **Mercadona**, and **La Carmela**.

    You can rename categories and supermarkets any time from the **Config** tab. For now, the defaults are enough to add your first product.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Add your first product">
    Tap the **Add Products** tab (the second icon in the navigation bar).

    1. Select a category from the category picker. Each category shows its color badge and name.
    2. Select a supermarket from the supermarket picker. Choose **Cualquier Supermercado** if the item is not store-specific.
    3. Type the product name in the text field.
    4. Tap **Add** to save it to your list.

    The product is saved to LocalStorage immediately. You do not need to confirm or save manually.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Browse your list">
    You have two ways to view your products:

    - **A-Z tab** — all products sorted alphabetically, regardless of category. Good for quickly finding a specific item.
    - **Categories tab** — products grouped by their category, each group showing the category color. Good for scanning a structured list.

    Both views respect the category visibility setting — categories toggled off in **Config** are hidden from both views.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Select products for your cart">
    In the **A-Z** or **Categories** tab, tap a product to select it for your shopping trip. Selected products are visually marked and will appear in the **Cart** tab.

    Tap a product again to deselect it. You can select as many products as you need before heading to the store.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Go shopping with the cart">
    Tap the **Cart** tab (the last icon in the navigation bar).

    The cart shows all selected products. Use the supermarket filter at the top to display only the products assigned to the store you are currently in. This lets you work through one store at a time without seeing irrelevant items.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Mark items as bought">
    In the **Cart** tab, tap a product to mark it as bought. Bought items are visually distinguished from items still to pick up.

    Tap a bought item again to unmark it if you need to.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Clear the list when you're done">
    In the **Cart** tab, tap **Limpiar Lista** (Clear list) in the top bar. A dialog lets you choose to clear only the purchased items, or all selected items at once.

    Your categories, supermarkets, and product definitions remain intact — only the `selected` and `done` state is reset.
  </Step>
</Steps>
